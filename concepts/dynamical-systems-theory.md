## Definition
Dynamical systems theory is the mathematical framework for describing how systems change over time. It provides tools for analyzing stability, attractors, bifurcations, and long-term behavior of systems governed by differential equations or maps.

## Key Concepts

### Phase Space
- State variables define a phase space
- Trajectories represent system evolution
- Attractors are long-term limit sets
- Basins of attraction partition phase space

### Stability Analysis
- Equilibrium points and their stability
- Linearization and eigenvalue analysis
- Lyapunov functions for global stability
- Structural stability and robustness

### Attractors
- Fixed points (steady states)
- Limit cycles (periodic oscillations)
- Tori (quasiperiodic motion)
- Strange attractors (chaos)

## Applications in Neuroscience

### Neural Mass Models
- [[neural mass model]]s are dynamical systems
- State variables: mean membrane potentials and firing rates
- Attractors correspond to brain states
- [[bifurcation analysis]] reveals state transitions

### Brain Dynamics
- brain oscillations as limit cycles
- Resting-state as attractor landscape
- Seizures as transitions between attractors
- [[whole brain]] models as coupled dynamical systems
