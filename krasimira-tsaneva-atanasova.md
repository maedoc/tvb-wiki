# Krasimira Tsaneva-Atanasova

**Krasimira Tsaneva-Atanasova** is a researcher working at the intersection of mathematics and computational neuroscience, with a focus on dynamical systems approaches to neural modeling [citation needed].

## Overview

Her research involves connecting dynamical systems theory with connectome-based whole-brain modeling [citation needed]. This includes work on mathematical frameworks for understanding complex brain dynamics, particularly neural mass models and dynamical systems analysis [citation needed].

## Research Focus

### Neural Mass Models and Dynamical Systems
Tsaneva-Atanasova's work centers on [[neural mass model|neural mass models]] (NMMs), which describe the collective behavior of large neuronal populations through averaged variables [citation needed]. She applies methods from [[dynamical systems theory]] and [[bifurcation theory]] to analyze how these models exhibit transitions between different dynamical regimes [citation needed].

### Epilepsy Modeling
Research contributions include computational modeling approaches to epilepsy [citation needed]. This involves mathematical analysis of seizure dynamics, including the [[Epileptor]] model and other slow-fast dynamical systems applied to seizure phenomenology [citation needed].

### Whole-Brain Network Dynamics
Work in this area explores how neural mass models behave when coupled into large-scale networks representing the [[connectome]] [citation needed]. Research examines how network structure influences emergent dynamics, including oscillatory patterns relevant to both healthy brain function and pathological states [citation needed].

### Mathematical Methods
Methodological contributions include [citation needed]:
- [[Bifurcation analysis|Bifurcation analysis]] of neural models
- Reduction techniques for complex neuronal systems
- Applications of [[mean-field theory]] to neural populations
