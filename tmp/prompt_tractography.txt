# Tractography

Tractography is a computational method for reconstructing white matter fiber pathways from diffusion MRI data, enabling non-invasive mapping of structural brain connectivity.

## Definition

Introduced by susumu-mori in 1999, tractography algorithms trace virtual "streamlines" through diffusion orientation fields to reconstruct the 3D trajectories of white matter fiber bundles.

## Methods

### Deterministic
- **Streamline tracking**: Follow principal diffusion direction
- **FACT**: Fiber Assignment by Continuous Tracking
- Fast but sensitive to noise and crossing fibers

### Probabilistic
- **Account for uncertainty**: Multiple possible pathways
- **Bootstrap**: Resample data to estimate distributions
- More robust but computationally intensive

### Advanced
- **CSD-based**: Use FOD from constrained spherical deconvolution
- **Global**: Optimize whole-brain tractograms simultaneously
- **Anatomically-constrained**: Use tissue boundaries to constrain tracking

## Challenges and Limitations

- **Crossing fibers**: Single tensor methods fail (constrained-spherical-deconvolution helps)
- **Validation**: Difficult to verify in vivo
- **False positives**: Spurious streamlines
- **False negatives**: Missed connections

## Role in Whole-Brain Modeling

Tractography produces the [[structural-connectivity]] matrices used as model inputs:

1. **Connection weights**: Streamline counts or FA values
2. **Fiber lengths**: For computing transmission delays
3. **Network topology**: Pattern of connections

## Quality Considerations

jones-2010 discusses validation challenges and best practices. Quality affects model reliability.

# Three-dimensional tracking of axonal projections in the brain by magnetic resonance imaging

**Authors:** Susumu Mori, Barbara J Crain, Vladimir P Chacko, Peter C van Zijl  
**Year:** 1999  
**Venue:** Annals of Neurology 45(2):265-269

## Summary
Introduced tractography algorithms for reconstructing white matter pathways from DTI data. Demonstrated 3D fiber tracking in the brain using diffusion anisotropy information.

## Key Contributions
- First DTI tractography algorithm
- Fiber tracking methodology
- 3D visualization of white matter pathways
- Foundation for connectomics

## Significance for Whole-Brain Modeling
Tractography provides the structural connectivity matrices used to constrain dynamic models. The accuracy of tractography directly affects model predictions.

**Author:** Derek K Jones

- Validation challenges for structural connectivity

Important caveats for interpreting structural connectivity data used in models. Understanding limitations helps assess uncertainty in model predictions derived from DTI data.

# Robust determination of the fibre orientation distribution in diffusion MRI

**Authors:** Jacques-Donald Tournier, Fernando Calamante, David G Gadian, Alan Connelly  
**Year:** 2007  
**Venue:** NeuroImage 35(4):1459-1472

## Summary
Introduced constrained spherical deconvolution (CSD) for resolving multiple fiber orientations within single voxels. Overcame limitations of DTI in regions with crossing fibers.

## Key Contributions
- Constrained spherical deconvolution (CSD)
- Resolution of crossing fibers
- Improved tractography in complex regions
- FOD (fibre orientation distribution) estimation

## Significance for Whole-Brain Modeling
Better structural connectivity estimates in crossing-fiber regions lead to more accurate network models. CSD is now standard in high-quality connectome reconstruction.
