# The Development of Human Functional Brain Networks

**Authors:** Jonathan D. Power, Damien A. Fair, et al.  
**Year:** 2010  
**Venue:** Neuron

## Key Contributions

- Comprehensive review of functional network development from infancy to adulthood
- Integration of structural and functional connectivity development
- Discussion of methodological considerations for developmental studies
- Framework for understanding typical and atypical development

## Abstract Summary

This review synthesizes evidence on how functional brain networks develop across childhood and adolescence. The authors discuss changes in network topology, hub organization, and module structure, relating these to underlying structural changes in white matter and synaptic pruning.

## Significance

Provides theoretical framework for understanding how large-scale brain networks develop, essential for developmental modeling and clinical applications.
