# Dynamic Mapping of Human Cortical Development During Childhood Through Early Adulthood

**Authors:** Nitin Gogtay, Jay N. Giedd, Leslie Lusk, et al.  
**Year:** 2004  
**Venue:** PNAS

## Key Contributions

- First longitudinal MRI study tracking cortical development from childhood to adulthood
- Demonstrated "back-to-front" pattern of cortical maturation (occipital to frontal)
- Showed that higher-order association cortices mature last
- Found that cortical thinning reflects pruning and myelination

## Abstract Summary

The authors conducted a longitudinal neuroimaging study of 13 children scanned repeatedly between ages 4-21. They found that cortical maturation follows a hierarchical pattern, with sensory and motor regions maturing first, followed by temporal and parietal association areas, and finally prefrontal cortex. This back-to-front gradient mirrors evolutionary cortical development.

## Significance

This landmark study established the spatial-temporal pattern of human cortical development, showing that phylogenetically newer regions mature later. Critical for understanding developmental windows and modeling age-appropriate brain networks.
