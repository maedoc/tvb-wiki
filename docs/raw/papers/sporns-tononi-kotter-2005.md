# The Human Connectome: A Structural Description of the Human Brain

**Authors:** Olaf Sporns, Giulio Tononi, Rolf Kötter  
**Year:** 2005  
**Venue:** PLoS Computational Biology

## Key Contributions

- Introduced the term "connectome" as a comprehensive structural description of the human brain
- Proposed that understanding connectivity is essential for understanding brain function
- Outlined technical requirements for mapping the human connectome
- Emphasized the importance of network analysis approaches

## Abstract Summary

The authors argue that understanding the structural organization of the brain is crucial for understanding its function. They propose the concept of the "connectome" - a comprehensive structural description of the network of elements and connections forming the human brain. The paper discusses the theoretical importance of the connectome and outlines potential approaches for mapping it.

## Significance

This is the seminal paper that introduced the term "connectome" to neuroscience, sparking the field of connectomics. The concept has since become central to modern neuroscience, driving major initiatives like the Human Connectome Project.
