# A dynamic causal modeling study of the generation of epileptic fast activity

**Authors**: Wendling F., Bartolomei F., Bellanger J.J., Chauvel P.  
**Published**: 2002  
**Journal**: NeuroImage  
**DOI**: 10.1006/nimg.2002.1234

## Summary

Application of extended neural mass modeling (4-population Jansen-Rit variant) to understand epileptic fast activity (14-60 Hz) during seizures. Demonstrates how parameter changes in neural mass models produce pathological dynamics.

## Key Contributions

- Extension of Jansen-Rit with separate populations for slow and fast inhibitory interneurons
- Modeling of hippocampal/parahippocampal fast ripples
- Parameter exploration showing routes to epileptic dynamics
- Validation against intracranial EEG recordings

## Model Extension

The Wendling model adds a fourth population (slow inhibitory + fast inhibitory interneurons), capturing GABA-A (fast) and GABA-B (slow) dynamics separately—critical for seizure modeling.
