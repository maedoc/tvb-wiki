# Supercomputers ready for use as discovery machines for neuroscience

**Authors**: Helias et al. (2012)
**Journal**: Frontiers in Neuroinformatics
**DOI**: https://doi.org/10.3389/fninf.2012.00026
**Full text**: https://doi.org/10.3389/fninf.2012.00026

## Summary

Helias and colleagues examine the readiness of contemporary supercomputing infrastructure for large-scale neuroscience simulation, demonstrating NEST's performance on flagship HPC systems. The paper benchmarks spiking network simulations across machines ranging from shared-memory workstations to massively parallel clusters, identifying scaling bottlenecks and hardware requirements for biologically detailed models. It argues that supercomputers are not merely speed-ups but enable qualitatively new scientific questions through sheer scale and complexity of simulatable networks. This work helped establish the roadmap for deploying NEST and similar tools on leadership-class computing facilities.

## Key Contributions

- NEST performance benchmarking on HPC systems
- Scalability across workstation to cluster environments
- Identification of scaling bottlenecks
- Hardware requirements for detailed models
- Roadmap for leadership-class computing deployment
