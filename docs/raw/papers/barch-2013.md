# Function in the Human Connectome: Task-fMRI and Individual Differences in Behavior

**Authors:** Deanna M. Barch, Gregory C. Burgess, et al.  
**Year:** 2013  
**Venue:** NeuroImage

## Key Contributions

- Description of HCP task-fMRI battery design
- Seven cognitive domains assessed: emotion, gambling, language, motor, relational, social, working memory
- Reliability assessment of task activations
- Initial individual differences analyses

## Abstract Summary

This paper describes the task-functional MRI component of the Human Connectome Project. The authors present a battery of seven tasks designed to assess a range of cognitive and affective domains, along with analyses of task activations and their relationships to individual differences in behavior.

## Significance

Provided a standardized task battery for large-scale functional neuroimaging. The tasks have been widely used and adapted by other researchers.
