# Normal Development of Brain Circuits

**Authors:** Ursula Tau, Susan E. Peterson  
**Year:** 2010  
**Venue:** Neuropsychology Review

## Key Contributions

- Comprehensive review of normal brain development from fetal period through adolescence
- Synthesis of structural and functional MRI findings on developmental trajectories
- Discussion of connectivity changes underlying cognitive development
- Integration of neuroimaging with histological studies

## Abstract Summary

This review synthesizes findings from structural and functional neuroimaging studies to characterize normal brain development. The authors discuss how brain circuits develop through childhood and adolescence, emphasizing changes in white matter integrity, cortical thickness, and functional connectivity patterns that underlie cognitive maturation.

## Significance

Provides foundational understanding of typical developmental trajectories essential for interpreting atypical development and modeling age-related changes in brain networks.
