# Theoretical Neuroscience: Computational and Mathematical Modeling of Neural Systems

**Authors**: Peter Dayan, Larry F. Abbott  
**Published**: 2001 (MIT Press)  
**ISBN**: 978-0-262-04199-7

## Summary

Foundational textbook covering computational neuroscience from single neurons to networks. Part III (Neural Coding and Decoding) and Part IV (Networks) establish the theoretical framework for neural mass modeling through mean-field approaches and population dynamics.

## Key Contributions

- Systematic treatment of integrate-and-fire neurons and firing-rate models
- Mathematical foundation for population density methods and mean-field theory
- Coverage of recurrent network dynamics and attractor states
- Connection between biophysical detail and simplified population models

## Relevance to Neural Mass Models

The firing-rate formulation in Chapter 7 provides the theoretical basis for Wilson-Cowan and Jansen-Rit type models, bridging detailed spiking networks and population-level approximations.
