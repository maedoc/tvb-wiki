# Maintenance, Reserve and Compensation: The Cognitive Neuroscience of Healthy Ageing

**Authors:** Roberto Cabeza et al.  
**Year:** 2018  
**Venue:** Nature Reviews Neuroscience

## Key Contributions

- Comprehensive framework distinguishing maintenance, reserve, and compensation
- Integration of multiple neuroimaging modalities
- Discussion of individual differences in aging trajectories
- Implications for interventions and prevention

## Abstract Summary

This review proposes a three-component framework for understanding cognitive aging: maintenance (preservation of brain structure/function), reserve (pre-existing capacity to cope with pathology), and compensation (recruitment of additional resources). The authors synthesize evidence for each mechanism and their interactions.

## Significance

Major theoretical advance providing clear definitions and distinctions between mechanisms of cognitive aging, essential for research and clinical applications.
