# Resting-State fMRI in the Human Connectome Project

**Authors:** Stephen M. Smith, David C. Van Essen, et al.  
**Year:** 2013  
**Venue:** NeuroImage

## Key Contributions

- Description of HCP resting-state fMRI acquisition protocols
- Group-level functional connectivity analysis in 200+ subjects
- Identification of major resting-state networks at high resolution
- Characterization of inter-subject variability

## Abstract Summary

This paper describes the resting-state fMRI component of the Human Connectome Project. The authors present group-level analyses of functional connectivity in over 200 subjects, identifying major resting-state networks at unprecedented spatial resolution and characterizing the variability across individuals.

## Significance

Established the resting-state fMRI protocols and analysis standards for the HCP. The high-resolution network maps have become reference datasets for the field.
