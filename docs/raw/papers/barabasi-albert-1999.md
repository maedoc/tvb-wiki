# Emergence of Scaling in Random Networks

**Authors:** Albert-László Barabási, Réka Albert  
**Year:** 1999  
**Venue:** Science

## Key Contributions

- Introduction of the scale-free network model
- Preferential attachment mechanism for network growth
- Demonstration of power-law degree distributions in real networks
- Implications for network robustness and vulnerability

## Abstract Summary

This paper demonstrates that many real-world networks exhibit scale-free degree distributions following a power law. The authors propose a mechanism of preferential attachment that explains how such networks emerge during growth.

## Significance

Introduced the concept of scale-free networks, which has been applied to brain connectivity. Brain networks show scale-free properties, with hub regions having many connections.
