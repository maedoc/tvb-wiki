# A low dimensional description of globally coupled heterogeneous neural networks of excitatory and inhibitory neurons

**Authors**: Roxana A. Stefanescu, Viktor K. Jirsa  
**Published**: 2008  
**Journal**: PLoS Computational Biology  
**DOI**: 10.1371/journal.pcbi.1000219

## Summary

Demonstrates how high-dimensional networks of heterogeneous spiking neurons can be reduced to low-dimensional neural mass descriptions through mean-field theory. Bridges detailed spiking networks and population models through systematic dimension reduction.

## Key Contributions

- Mean-field reduction from spiking network to rate-based equations
- Handling of heterogeneity in neuron parameters
- Derivation of macroscopic order parameters from microscopic dynamics
- Mathematical bridge between NEST-style and TVB-style modeling

## Method

Uses Ott-Antonsen ansatz and moment closure techniques to derive closed-form equations for population firing rates from networks of quadratic integrate-and-fire neurons with distributed parameters.
