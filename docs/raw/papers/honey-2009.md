# Predicting Human Resting-State Functional Connectivity from Structural Connectivity

**Authors:** Christopher J. Honey, Olaf Sporns, Leila Cammoun, Xavier Gigandet, Jean-Philippe Thiran, Reto Meuli, Patric Hagmann  
**Year:** 2009  
**Venue:** PNAS

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrated predictability of functional connectivity from structural connectivity
- Used diffusion MRI and resting-state fMRI in the same subjects
- Showed that indirect connections contribute to functional connectivity
- Established the SC-FC relationship framework

## Abstract Summary

This study directly compares structural connectivity from diffusion MRI to resting-state functional connectivity in the same individuals. The authors show that functional connectivity can be partially predicted from structural connectivity, but indirect connections and non-structural factors also contribute.

## Significance

A landmark study establishing the relationship between structural and functional connectivity. Demonstrated that while structure constrains function, the relationship is not one-to-one, motivating whole-brain modeling approaches.
