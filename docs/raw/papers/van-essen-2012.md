# The Human Connectome Project: A Data Acquisition Perspective

**Authors:** David C. Van Essen, Kamil Ugurbil, et al.  
**Year:** 2012  
**Venue:** NeuroImage

## Key Contributions

- Detailed description of HCP imaging protocols and hardware optimization
- Customized 3T and 7T scanner configurations for high-resolution imaging
- Quality assurance procedures for multimodal data acquisition
- Strategies for minimizing artifacts and maximizing data quality

## Abstract Summary

This paper describes the data acquisition strategies employed by the Human Connectome Project. The authors detail the customized 3T and 7T MRI systems, optimized pulse sequences, and quality assurance procedures designed to maximize the quality of structural, functional, and diffusion MRI data.

## Significance

Provides the technical foundation for HCP data acquisition. The protocols described here have been widely adopted and adapted by other research groups.
