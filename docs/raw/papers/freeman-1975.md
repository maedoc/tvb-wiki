# Mass Action in the Nervous System

**Author**: Walter J. Freeman  
**Published**: 1975 (Academic Press)  
**ISBN**: 978-0-12-262350-2

## Summary

Pioneering work establishing neural mass modeling as a quantitative framework. Freeman developed the KII and KIII models describing collective neural activity through nonlinear dynamics, with direct application to EEG/olfactory system analysis.

## Key Contributions

- K-set hierarchy: K0 (single neuron), KI (population), KII (coupled populations), KIII (multisensory integration)
- Nonlinear dynamics of excitatory-inhibitory interactions
- Application to olfactory bulb and EEG generation
- Foundation for later population-based brain models

## Historical Significance

Freeman's models predate and influenced Wilson-Cowan, Jansen-Rit, and modern neural mass implementations in TVB. The concept of mesoscopic population dynamics emerged from this work.
