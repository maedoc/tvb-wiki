# Methods in Neuronal Modeling: From Synapses to Networks

**Editors**: Christof Koch, Idan Segev  
**Published**: 1998 (MIT Press, 2nd Edition)  
**ISBN**: 978-0-262-61120-5

## Summary

Classic edited volume on computational modeling methods. The chapter by Abbott on "Lapique's Introduction of the Integrate-and-Fire Model Neuron" and Rall's work on cable theory provide historical and technical foundations for neural population modeling.

## Key Contributions

- Single-compartment and multi-compartment modeling techniques
- Synaptic integration and neuronal computation
- Network modeling approaches from 1990s perspective
- Balance of biological detail and computational tractability

## Historical Context

This volume appeared during the transition from single-neuron Hodgkin-Huxley simulations to network models, setting the stage for later population-level simplifications.
