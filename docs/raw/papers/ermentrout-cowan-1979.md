# Mathematical studies of visual hallucination patterns

**Authors**: G. Bard Ermentrout, Jack D. Cowan  
**Published**: 1979  
**Journal**: Biological Cybernetics  
**DOI**: 10.1007/BF00336963

## Summary

Classic application of neural field theory to explain geometric visual hallucinations (form constants) experienced during flicker stimulation, drug effects, or migraine auras. Uses spatially extended Wilson-Cowan dynamics to predict hallucination patterns.

## Key Contributions

- Linear stability analysis of neural fields
- Prediction of characteristic hallucination geometries (tunnels, spirals, cobwebs)
- Connection to visual cortex retinotopic organization
- Early demonstration of pattern formation in neural tissue

## Mechanism

Localized excitation combined with surround inhibition produces Turing-like instabilities with specific spatial frequencies matching hallucination patterns. The eigenfunctions of the linearized field equations correspond to perceived form constants.
