# Genome-wide Association Studies of Brain Imaging Phenotypes from UK Biobank

**Authors:** Lloyd T. Elliott, Luca T. Tallavaara, et al.  
**Year:** 2018  
**Venue:** Nature

## Key Contributions

- First large-scale GWAS of brain imaging phenotypes
- Identified genetic associations with brain structure and connectivity
- Integration of multimodal imaging with genetic data
- Discovery of novel loci associated with brain phenotypes

## Abstract Summary

This paper reports the first genome-wide association studies of brain imaging phenotypes using UK Biobank data. The authors identify numerous genetic loci associated with brain structure, white matter integrity, and functional connectivity, providing insights into the genetic architecture of brain organization.

## Significance

Pioneered the integration of large-scale genetics and neuroimaging. Demonstrated the power of population imaging for genetic discovery and paved the way for imaging genetics studies.
