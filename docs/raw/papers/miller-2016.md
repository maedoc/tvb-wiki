# Multimodal Population Brain Imaging in the UK Biobank: Prospective Epidemiological Study

**Authors:** Karla L. Miller, Fidel Alfaro-Almagro, et al.  
**Year:** 2016  
**Venue:** The Lancet

## Key Contributions

- Description of UK Biobank brain imaging protocol
- Multimodal imaging: structural MRI, fMRI, dMRI
- Integration with extensive phenotypic and genetic data
- Quality control and processing pipelines

## Abstract Summary

This paper introduces the UK Biobank brain imaging study, which aims to image 100,000 participants with multimodal MRI. The authors describe the imaging protocol, quality control procedures, and initial data release, demonstrating the feasibility of large-scale population neuroimaging.

## Significance

The UK Biobank Imaging Study is the largest brain imaging study ever conducted. The multimodal data combined with genetic and health information enables unprecedented population-level analyses of brain structure and function.
