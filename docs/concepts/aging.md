# Brain Aging

Brain aging involves structural and functional changes across the lifespan, from early adulthood through senescence. These changes follow non-linear trajectories and vary substantially across individuals.

## Definition

Brain aging encompasses the progressive changes in brain structure, function, and connectivity that occur from early adulthood onward. These changes include both normative declines and compensatory adaptations that enable maintained cognitive function.

## Structural Changes

### Cortical Changes
- **Cortical thinning**: Gradual reduction in cortical thickness
- **Regional variation**: Some regions more affected than others
- **Non-linear trajectories**: Rates of change vary by age

### White Matter Changes
- **White matter hyperintensities**: Increased with age
- **Fractional anisotropy decline**: Reduced white matter integrity
- **Tract-specific patterns**: Some pathways more vulnerable

### Ventricular Enlargement
- Lateral ventricles expand with age
- Reflects brain volume loss
- Marker of accelerated aging

## Functional Changes

### Default Mode Network
- DMN connectivity decreases with age
- Posterior cingulate-medial prefrontal correlation weakens
- Correlates with cognitive performance
- Potential early marker of cognitive decline

### Compensation Mechanisms
- Increased bilateral recruitment
- Additional brain regions engaged
- Alternative strategies for task performance

## Theoretical Frameworks

### Maintenance, Reserve, and Compensation (Cabeza et al., 2018)

1. **Brain Maintenance**: Preservation of brain structure and function
   - Individuals with less age-related brain change
   - Lifestyle and genetic factors

2. **Cognitive Reserve**: Pre-existing capacity to cope with pathology
   - Passive: Brain reserve (structural)
   - Active: Compensation mechanisms

3. **Compensation**: Recruitment of additional resources
   - Reorganization of function
   - Alternative neural strategies

## Individual Differences

Aging trajectories vary due to:
- **Genetics**: Apolipoprotein E (APOE) status
- **Education**: Cognitive reserve factor
- **Cardiovascular health**: Vascular contributions
- **Lifestyle**: Physical activity, cognitive engagement
- **Social engagement**: Protective factors

## Role in Whole-Brain Modeling

### Age-Related Parameters
- Slower synaptic dynamics
- Altered connection strengths
- Changed network topology
- Reduced connection density

### Applications
- Predicting cognitive decline
- Modeling dementia progression
- Understanding successful aging
- Designing interventions
