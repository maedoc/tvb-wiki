# Bharat Biswal

**Born:** 1963  
**Nationality:** Indian-American  
**Institution:** New Jersey Medical School, Rutgers University

## Overview
Bharat Biswal is a biomedical engineer who discovered resting-state functional connectivity in 1995. His seminal paper showed that spontaneous low-frequency fluctuations in fMRI data reflect functional connections between brain regions, launching the field of resting-state fMRI.

## Key Contributions
- **1995**: Discovered resting-state functional connectivity
- Demonstrated bilateral motor cortex correlation during rest
- Established the phenomenon of intrinsic connectivity networks
- Developed methods for resting-state data analysis

## Significance
Resting-state functional connectivity is the primary empirical target for whole-brain models. Biswal's discovery provided the phenomenon that models like TVB seek to explain through spontaneous activity dynamics constrained by structural connectivity.
