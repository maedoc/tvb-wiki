# Nancy Andrews-Hanna (Jessica R. Andrews-Hanna)

Cognitive neuroscientist specializing in the default mode network, aging, and internally-directed cognition.

## Key Contributions

- First systematic study of DMN connectivity changes in aging (2007)
- Comprehensive anatomical and functional characterization of DMN (2008)
- Demonstrated DMN fractionation into distinct subsystems (2010)
- Established DMN role in self-referential processing and mind-wandering

## Major Work

### DMN and Aging (2007)
- Found reduced DMN connectivity correlates with cognitive decline
- Demonstrated specific vulnerability of DMN to aging
- Established DMN as marker of brain aging

### DMN Anatomy Review (2008)
- Comprehensive review of DMN anatomy and function
- Integration of functional imaging and neuroanatomy
- Discussion of DMN in disease

### DMN Fractionation (2010)
- Showed DMN comprises distinct subsystems
- Identified dorsal medial prefrontal and medial temporal subsystems
- Demonstrated different functional roles of DMN components

## Research Focus
- Default mode network organization
- Cognitive aging and dementia
- Internally-directed cognition (mind-wandering, memory)
- Self-referential processing
