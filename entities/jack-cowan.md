# Jack D. Cowan (1933-2021)

British mathematical biologist and computational neuroscientist. Co-creator of the Wilson-Cowan model and founding figure in mathematical neuroscience. Professor at University of Chicago for over 50 years, profoundly influenced generations of computational neuroscientists.

## Key Contributions

- **Wilson-Cowan model (1972, 1973)**: With Hugh Wilson, created the canonical neural population model
- **Neural field theory**: Spatial extension modeling pattern formation and traveling waves
- **Visual hallucination theory**: With Bard Ermentrout, used neural fields to explain geometric hallucinations
- **Mathematical neuroscience education**: Trained and mentored numerous leaders in the field

## Major Publications

- Wilson & Cowan (1972) "Excitatory and inhibitory interactions in localized populations of model neurons"
- Wilson & Cowan (1973) "A mathematical theory of the functional dynamics of cortical and thalamic nervous tissue"
- Ermentrout & Cowan (1979) "A mathematical theory of visual hallucinations"
- Cowan et al. (2016) "Wiring the brain through evolution"

## Impact

The Wilson-Cowan model is the most cited and widely used neural population model. It established:
- Firing-rate approximation of spiking neurons
- Excitatory-inhibitory circuit dynamics
- Connection between population dynamics and oscillatory behavior
- Mathematical foundation for later neural mass and mean-field models
