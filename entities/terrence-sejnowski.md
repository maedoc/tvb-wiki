# Terrence J. Sejnowski

American computational neuroscientist. Francis Crick Professor at Salk Institute, UC San Diego, and Howard Hughes Medical Institute Investigator. One of the most influential computational neuroscientists, bridging machine learning, neural modeling, and brain theory.

## Key Contributions

- **Computational neuroscience**: Pioneering work linking neural dynamics to computation
- **Neural population dynamics**: Mean-field analysis and population coding
- **Neural network learning**: Boltzmann machines, backpropagation, and learning algorithms
- **Independent component analysis**: Application to EEG and fMRI signal separation

## Major Publications

- Sejnowski & Destexhe (2009) "Wilson-Cowan model" (Scholarpedia review)
- Churchland & Sejnowski (1992) "The Computational Brain"
- Dayan & Abbott (2001) "Theoretical Neuroscience" (with Peter Dayan)
- Numerous papers on neural dynamics, learning, and brain computation

## Impact on Neural Mass Modeling

Sejnowski's work, particularly through textbooks (Dayan & Abbott) and reviews (Destexhe & Sejnowski on Wilson-Cowan), has educated generations of researchers in population-level brain modeling. His work on independent component analysis has also enabled better preprocessing of EEG/MEG data used in neural mass model validation.
