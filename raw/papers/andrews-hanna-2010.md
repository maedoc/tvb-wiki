# Functional-Anatomic Fractionation of the Brain's Default Network

**Authors:** Jessica R. Andrews-Hanna, Jay S. Reidler, et al.  
**Year:** 2010  
**Venue:** Neuron

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrated DMN consists of distinct functional subsystems
- Identified dorsal medial prefrontal subsystem and medial temporal lobe subsystem
- Showed different functional roles of DMN components
- Established hierarchical organization within DMN

## Abstract Summary

Using resting-state fMRI and meta-analysis, the authors showed the default mode network comprises two distinct subsystems: a dorsal medial prefrontal subsystem involved in self-relevant mentalizing, and a medial temporal lobe subsystem involved in episodic memory and future simulation.

## Significance

Important refinement of DMN organization, showing it is not a unitary network but comprises functionally distinct subsystems with different connectivity patterns.
