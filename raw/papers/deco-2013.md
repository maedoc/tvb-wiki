# Resting brains never rest: computational insights into potential cognitive architectures

**Authors**: Deco et al. (2013)
**Journal**: Trends in Neurosciences
**DOI**: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tins.2013.09.002
**Full text**: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tins.2013.09.002

## Summary

This review uses large-scale computational brain models to explore how spontaneous resting-state activity is generated and what it reveals about the brain's underlying cognitive architecture. The authors demonstrate that noise-driven fluctuations around a stable fixed point in a structured network—constrained by empirical structural connectivity—can reproduce empirical resting-state functional connectivity patterns. The paper argues that the resting brain continuously explores a repertoire of functional states that overlap with task-evoked activations, suggesting a unified view of resting and task dynamics. These computational insights have significantly influenced theories of intrinsic brain activity and the role of structural connectivity in shaping function.

## Key Contributions

- Computational models of resting-state activity
- Noise-driven fluctuations reproduce functional connectivity
- Repertoire of functional states theory
- Unified view of resting and task dynamics
