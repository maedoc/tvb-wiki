## Abstract

In a traditional passive optical network (PON), the optical network units (ONUs) remain in an active state for the whole time of every time cycle, i.e., 100% time in active mode of every time cycle. As a result, a considerable amount of energy is wasted. This paper analyzes the energy efficiency of the Smart Bandwidth Reduction and Redistribution Allocation (SBRRA) algorithm, where ONUs are in active mode only during their transmission time based on this algorithm; otherwise, they are in sleep mode. The performance of the energy efficiency of the SBRRA algorithm is evaluated using a numerical simulation model in comparison with the existing delay and energy efficient dynamic bandwidth allocation (DEE-DBA) algorithm and a traditional bandwidth allocation algorithm in the time-division multiplexing Ethernet-PON (TDM EPON). The SBRRA algorithm saved 14.28% and 12.86% energy even at 100% network load than DEEDBA and Traditional EPON for 1.0 ms and 1.5 ms cycle-time, respectively. So, the SBRRA algorithm ensures energy efficient application in TDM EPON.
