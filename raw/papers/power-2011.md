# Functional Network Organization of the Human Brain

**Authors:** Jonathan D. Power, et al.  
**Year:** 2011  
**Venue:** Neuron

## Key Contributions

- Comprehensive mapping of resting-state functional networks
- Identification of major functional systems in the brain
- Demonstration of network organization across multiple spatial scales
- Methods for network identification and characterization

## Abstract Summary

This study provides a detailed mapping of the human brain's functional network organization using resting-state fMRI. The authors identify major functional networks including default mode, attention, sensorimotor, and visual networks, and characterize their spatial organization.

## Significance

A comprehensive characterization of human functional network architecture. The network definitions and methods have been widely adopted in the field.
