# Brain magnetic resonance imaging with contrast dependent on blood oxygenation

**Authors:** Seiji Ogawa, Tso-Ming Lee, Alan R Kay, David W Tank  
**Year:** 1990  
**Venue:** PNAS 87(24):9868-9872

## Summary
Landmark paper demonstrating that MRI signal contrast can be generated by blood oxygenation level-dependent (BOLD) effects. Showed that changes in blood oxygenation alter local magnetic field properties, enabling non-invasive imaging of brain function.

## Key Contributions
- Discovered BOLD contrast mechanism for fMRI
- Demonstrated that deoxyhemoglobin acts as an endogenous contrast agent
- Established foundation for functional neuroimaging

## Significance for Whole-Brain Modeling
The BOLD signal is the primary output measure for validating whole-brain models against empirical data. Understanding the neurovascular coupling that produces BOLD is essential for model-data comparison.
