# Neuronal Dynamics: From Single Neurons to Networks and Models of Cognition

**Authors**: Wulfram Gerstner, Werner M. Kistler, Richard Naud, Liam Paninski  
**Published**: 2014 (Cambridge University Press)  
**ISBN**: 978-1-107-06083-6

## Summary

Modern textbook connecting single-neuron biophysics to network dynamics and cognitive models. Part IV explicitly covers population dynamics and rate models essential for whole-brain modeling.

## Key Contributions

- Unified treatment from Hodgkin-Huxley to rate-based population models
- Population activity equations and mean-field theory
- Dynamic equations for synaptic conductances
- Bridging detailed spiking to reduced population models

## Relevance to TVB-style Modeling

Chapter 14 (Population Models) derives the rate-based equations used in neural mass models, showing how synaptic dynamics translate to population firing rates—a core assumption in Jansen-Rit and related models.
