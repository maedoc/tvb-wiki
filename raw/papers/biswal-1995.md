# Functional connectivity in the motor cortex of resting human brain using echo-planar MRI

**Authors:** Bharat B Biswal, Frank Z Yetkin, Victor M Haughton, James S Hyde  
**Year:** 1995  
**Venue:** Magnetic Resonance in Medicine 34(4):537-541

## Summary
First demonstration of spontaneous low-frequency correlations in resting-state fMRI data. Showed that left and right motor cortices are functionally connected even in the absence of task, launching the field of resting-state functional connectivity.

## Key Contributions
- Discovered resting-state functional connectivity in humans
- Demonstrated bilateral motor cortex correlation at rest
- Established foundation for intrinsic connectivity networks

## Significance for Whole-Brain Modeling
Resting-state connectivity is the primary target for whole-brain models. This paper established the empirical phenomenon that models seek to explain through spontaneous activity and structural connectivity constraints.
