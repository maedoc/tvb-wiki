# Model of brain rhythmic activity: the alpha rhythm of the thalamus

**Authors**: Fernando Lopes da Silva, A. Hoeks, H.A. Smits, L.H. Zetterberg  
**Published**: 1974  
**Journal**: Kybernetik  
**DOI**: 10.1007/BF00270757

## Summary

First neural mass model specifically designed to reproduce EEG rhythms—particularly the alpha (8-13 Hz) oscillations generated by thalamocortical circuits. Established the template for subsequent EEG-focused population models.

## Key Contributions

- Coupled excitatory-inhibitory population model for thalamocortical circuits
- Reproduction of alpha rhythm characteristics (frequency, amplitude, wave morphology)
- Linear and nonlinear analysis of oscillatory regimes
- Foundation for Jansen-Rit model architecture

## Model Architecture

Two interconnected populations (excitatory thalamocortical relay cells + inhibitory interneurons) with recurrent feedback loops producing rhythmic activity through inhibitory post-synaptic potential (IPSP) feedback.
