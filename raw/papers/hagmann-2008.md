# Mapping the Structural Core of Human Cerebral Cortex

**Authors:** Patric Hagmann, Leila Cammoun, Xavier Gigandet, Reto Meuli, Christopher J. Honey, Van J. Wedeen, Olaf Sporns  
**Year:** 2008  
**Venue:** PLoS Biology

## Key Contributions

- Identified a structural core of highly interconnected regions in posterior medial and parietal cortex
- Demonstrated dense interconnectivity between core regions using diffusion MRI and graph analysis
- Showed the core's central role in information integration
- Established relationship between structural connectivity and resting-state functional connectivity

## Abstract Summary

Using diffusion spectrum imaging and graph theory analysis, the authors identified a structural core of highly interconnected regions in the human cerebral cortex. This core, located in posterior medial and parietal cortex, contains the majority of connector hubs and shows high efficiency for information transfer. Core regions are also strongly functionally coupled.

## Significance

This study provided early evidence for a "rich club" organization in the human brain - a set of highly interconnected hub regions that form a structural backbone. This concept has become central to understanding brain network organization.
