## Abstract

: Major depressive disorder (MDD) is one of the most prevalent mental illnesses worldwide. Its complex neurobiological mechanisms, coupled with the urgent need for personalized treatment, have driven the rapid development of neuroimaging and big data research in MDD. Resting-state functional magnetic resonance imaging, a noninvasive whole-brain neuroimaging technique, has been widely used to study MDD-related brain features. However, the generalizability of previous findings has been limited by small sample sizes and methodological inconsistencies. To address this, Chinese researchers, in collaboration with psychiatric experts nationwide, established the Depression Imaging REsearch ConsorTium (DIRECT) based on the national clinical user network of DPARSF. By implementing a unified standardized preprocessing pipeline, the consortium has substantially improved the reproducibility and comparability of research findings. The REST-meta-MDD database, publicly released by the DIRECT team in 2020, provides an open resource for global researchers. This study presents a bibliometric analysis of 103 publications (2019 – 2025) that utilized the REST-meta-MDD database. The results demonstrate that the dataset has accelerated MDD neuroimaging research, facilitated international collaborations, and promoted the development of open science. Through data sharing, researchers have made notable advances in MDD subtype classification, diagnostic model optimization, and the exploration of brain structural and functional heterogeneity. Furthermore, it has provided insights for the diagnosis of other mental disorders, enhancing diagnostic differentiation between neuropsychiatric diseases. In the future, with the inclusion of more samples and the advancement of data sharing, the database is expected to play an increasingly important role in training multi-disease classification, facilitating cross-disease diagnostics, guiding neuromodulation interventions, and enabling AI-based analyses targeting specific clinical problems
