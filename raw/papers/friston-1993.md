# Functional Connectivity: The Principal-Component Analysis of Large (PET and fMRI) Data Sets

**Authors:** Karl J. Friston, Chris D. Frith, Peter F. Liddle, Richard S. J. Frackowiak  
**Year:** 1993  
**Venue:** Journal of Cerebral Blood Flow and Metabolism

## Key Contributions

- Early definition of functional connectivity in neuroimaging
- Application of principal component analysis to brain data
- Demonstration of distributed networks in PET data
- Foundation for later resting-state connectivity studies

## Abstract Summary

This paper introduces the concept of functional connectivity in neuroimaging, defined as the temporal correlation between spatially remote neurophysiological events. The authors apply principal component analysis to PET and fMRI data to identify functionally connected networks.

## Significance

One of the earliest papers to formally define and study functional connectivity in neuroimaging. Established the conceptual framework that underlies modern resting-state functional connectivity research.
