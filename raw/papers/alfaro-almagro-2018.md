# Image Processing and Quality Control for the First 100,000 Brain Imaging Datasets from UK Biobank

**Authors:** Fidel Alfaro-Almagro, Mark Jenkinson, et al.  
**Year:** 2018  
**Venue:** NeuroImage

## Key Contributions

- Comprehensive processing pipelines for UK Biobank imaging data
- Automated quality control procedures for large-scale datasets
- Standardized structural, functional, and diffusion processing
- Public release of processed data and QC metrics

## Abstract Summary

This paper describes the image processing and quality control pipelines developed for the UK Biobank brain imaging study. The authors present automated procedures for processing structural, functional, and diffusion MRI data from 100,000 participants, along with extensive quality control metrics.

## Significance

Established the processing standards for the world's largest brain imaging dataset. The pipelines and QC procedures have enabled thousands of researchers to use UK Biobank imaging data.
