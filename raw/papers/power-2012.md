# Spurious but systematic correlations in functional connectivity MRI networks arise from subject motion

**Authors:** Jonathan D Power, Kelly A Barnes, Abraham Z Snyder, Bradley L Schlaggar, Marcus E Raichle  
**Year:** 2012  
**Venue:** NeuroImage 59(3):2142-2154

## Summary
Demonstrated that head motion during resting-state fMRI creates systematic artifacts that inflate functional connectivity estimates, particularly affecting long-range connections. Led to widespread adoption of motion correction strategies.

## Key Contributions
- Identified motion as major confound in resting-state connectivity
- Motion artifact has distance-dependent structure
- Influenced preprocessing pipelines globally

## Significance for Whole-Brain Modeling
Critical for understanding data quality issues in empirical connectivity matrices used to constrain or validate models. Motion artifacts must be considered when comparing model outputs to resting-state data.
