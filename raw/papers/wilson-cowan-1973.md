# A mathematical theory of the functional dynamics of cortical and thalamic nervous tissue

**Authors**: Hugh R. Wilson, Jack D. Cowan  
**Published**: 1973  
**Journal**: Kybernetik  
**DOI**: 10.1007/BF00288786

## Summary

Extension of the Wilson-Cowan model to include spatial structure, creating the Wilson-Cowan neural field theory. This paper introduces spatially extended population equations capable of generating traveling waves and pattern formation in cortical tissue.

## Key Contributions

- Spatially extended neural field equations
- Analysis of traveling waves and spatial patterns
- Application to cortical spreading depression and hallucination patterns
- Mathematical foundation for modern neural field models used in TVB

## Spatial Extension

The model adds spatial coupling via convolution with connectivity kernels:
- ∂E/∂t = -E + S_E(∫w_EE(r-r')E(r')dr' - ∫w_EI(r-r')I(r')dr' + P)
- ∂I/∂t = -I + S_I(∫w_IE(r-r')E(r')dr' - ∫w_II(r-r')I(r')dr' + Q)
