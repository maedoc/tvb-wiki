# Structural Brain Changes in Aging: Courses, Causes and Cognitive Consequences

**Authors:** Anders M. Fjell, Kristine B. Walhovd  
**Year:** 2010  
**Venue:** Reviews in the Neurosciences

## Key Contributions

- Comprehensive review of structural MRI findings in aging
- Discussion of non-linear trajectories of brain change
- Integration of genetic and lifestyle factors
- Link between structural changes and cognition

## Abstract Summary

This review examines structural brain changes in aging, including cortical thinning, white matter decline, and ventricular expansion. The authors discuss how these changes follow non-linear trajectories and are influenced by genetics, cardiovascular health, and lifestyle factors.

## Significance

Important synthesis of structural neuroimaging in aging, establishing trajectories of change and their cognitive correlates.
