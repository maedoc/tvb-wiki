# The organization of the human cerebellum estimated by intrinsic functional connectivity

**Authors:** Xiao-Ning Zuo et al.  
**Year:** 2010  
**Venue:** Journal of Neurophysiology 106(5):2322-2345

## Summary
Investigated test-retest reliability of intrinsic connectivity networks (ICNs) across multiple sessions. Demonstrated that resting-state networks are reproducible and reliable measures of brain organization.

## Key Contributions
- Test-retest reliability of resting-state networks
- Temporal stability of intrinsic connectivity
- Methodological standards for rs-fMRI reliability

## Significance for Whole-Brain Modeling
Reliability of resting-state data is crucial for using empirical connectivity to constrain models. If connectivity patterns are not reproducible, they cannot serve as valid model targets.
