# The Brain's Default Network: Anatomy, Function, and Relevance to Disease

**Authors:** Randy L. Buckner, Jessica R. Andrews-Hanna, Daniel L. Schacter  
**Year:** 2008  
**Venue:** Annals of the New York Academy of Sciences

## Key Contributions

- Comprehensive anatomical description of the default mode network
- Integration of function and anatomy of DMN regions
- Discussion of DMN role in self-referential processing and memory
- Review of DMN alterations in neurological and psychiatric disease

## Abstract Summary

This review provides a detailed anatomical and functional characterization of the default mode network. The authors discuss the core DMN regions (medial prefrontal cortex, posterior cingulate cortex, angular gyrus, hippocampus), their anatomical connections, and their role in internally-directed cognition.

## Significance

Foundational review establishing the anatomical basis of the DMN and its functional significance, essential reference for DMN research.
