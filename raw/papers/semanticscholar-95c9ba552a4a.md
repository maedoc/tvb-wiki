## Abstract

Raman spectroscopy and surface-enhanced Raman scattering (SERS) are widely used across chemistry, materials science, environmental monitoring, and biosensing, yet researchers continue to face persistent challenges in reproducibility, interoperability, and workflow fragmentation. To alleviate these concerns and identify community priorities, 110 semistructured interviews were conducted across academic and industrial users. The results highlighted dominant needs for nocode accessibility, transparent and trustworthy workflows, controlled data sharing, and standardized spectral data and metadata practices. In response, SpectraGuru, an open-source, web-based platform designed to support standardized Raman and SERS preprocessing, analysis, and visualization through an accessible browser interface has been developed. The current implementation consolidates common analytical workflows into a unified, non-code environment, directly addressing usability and workflow fragmentation. While current capabilities mitigate several high-impact challenges, important gaps remain in data governance, interoperability, and scalable AI evaluation. In this publication, an outline for future directions toward a community-driven, AI-enabled Raman and SERS cyberinfrastructure, encompassing governed data backends, modular AI analytics, and assistant-supported user interaction is detailed. This staged approach can position SpectraGuru as both an immediate solution for current workflows and a foundation for sustainable, reproducible spectroscopy research and broader applications.
