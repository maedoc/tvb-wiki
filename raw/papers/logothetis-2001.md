# Neurophysiological investigation of the basis of the fMRI signal

**Authors:** Nikos K Logothetis, Jon Pauls, Mark Augath, Torsten Trinath, Ernst Otermann  
**Year:** 2001  
**Venue:** Nature 412(6843):150-157

## Summary
Simultaneous recording of intracortical neural activity and fMRI signals in monkeys to establish the neurophysiological basis of BOLD. Demonstrated that BOLD signal correlates most strongly with local field potentials (LFPs) rather than multi-unit spiking activity.

## Key Contributions
- BOLD signal reflects input and intracortical processing (LFPs) more than output spiking
- Established critical link between neural activity and hemodynamic response
- Informed forward models for neural mass model to BOLD coupling

## Significance for Whole-Brain Modeling
Provides empirical basis for coupling neural mass model outputs to simulated BOLD signals. The LFP-BOLD relationship underlies how TVB and similar platforms generate synthetic fMRI data.
