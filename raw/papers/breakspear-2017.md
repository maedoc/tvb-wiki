# Dynamic models of large-scale brain activity

**Author**: Michael Breakspear  
**Published**: 2017  
**Journal**: Nature Neuroscience (Review)  
**DOI**: 10.1038/s41593-017-0015-4

## Summary

Landmark review paper synthesizing dynamic models of large-scale brain activity. Covers neural mass models, network dynamics, and clinical applications. Essential reading for whole-brain modeling connecting structure to function through dynamical systems theory.

## Key Contributions

- Taxonomy of brain models: mass, network, and neural field models
- Mathematical framework linking structural connectivity to functional dynamics
- Role of time delays and network topology in emergent oscillations
- Clinical applications to epilepsy, schizophrenia, and consciousness

## Relevance to TVB

Explicitly discusses TVB-style modeling and the Virtual Brain platform as exemplifying the integration of structural connectivity with neural mass dynamics for personalized brain simulation.
