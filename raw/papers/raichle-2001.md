# A Default Mode of Brain Function

**Authors:** Marcus E. Raichle, Abraham Z. Snyder, et al.  
**Year:** 2001  
**Venue:** PNAS

## Key Contributions

- Introduced the concept of "default mode" brain function
- Identified brain regions active during rest and deactivated during tasks
- Proposed functional significance of intrinsic brain activity
- Established foundation for resting-state fMRI research

## Abstract Summary

The authors observed that specific brain regions are consistently active when the brain is at rest and deactivate during goal-directed tasks. They termed this the "default mode of brain function" and proposed it represents a baseline state of organized, intrinsic activity.

## Significance

Seminal paper that launched the field of resting-state functional connectivity and the study of the default mode network. One of the most cited papers in neuroscience.
