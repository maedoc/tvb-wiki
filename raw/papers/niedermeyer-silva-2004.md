# Electroencephalography: Basic Principles, Clinical Applications, and Related Fields (5th Edition)

**Authors:** Ernst Niedermeyer, Fernando HL da Silva (eds)  
**Year:** 2004 (5th ed)  
**Venue:** Lippincott Williams & Wilkins

## Summary
Definitive textbook on EEG covering physical principles, normal and abnormal patterns, clinical applications, and advanced techniques. Standard reference for EEG methodology and interpretation.

## Key Contributions
- Comprehensive EEG physics and physiology
- Normal and pathological EEG patterns
- Clinical and research applications
- Technical standards for EEG recording

## Significance for Whole-Brain Modeling
Essential reference for understanding EEG signal generation, which is a primary output of neural mass models. Informs model validation against empirical EEG patterns.
