# Pushing Spatial and Temporal Resolution for Functional and Diffusion MRI in the Human Connectome Project

**Authors:** Kamil Ugurbil, David C. Van Essen, et al.  
**Year:** 2013  
**Venue:** NeuroImage

## Key Contributions

- Technical innovations for high-resolution fMRI and dMRI at 3T and 7T
- Multiband acceleration techniques for faster acquisition
- Optimization of spatial and temporal resolution trade-offs
- Quality assessment of high-resolution imaging

## Abstract Summary

This paper describes the technical innovations in the HCP that enable high spatial and temporal resolution for functional and diffusion MRI. The authors detail multiband acceleration techniques, coil configurations, and pulse sequence optimizations that push the boundaries of MRI resolution.

## Significance

Demonstrated that high-resolution multimodal imaging is feasible in large-scale studies. The multiband techniques described here have been widely adopted beyond the HCP.
