# The Cognitive Neuroscience of Ageing and Functional Reserve

**Author:** Cheryl L. Grady  
**Year:** 2012  
**Venue:** European Journal of Neuroscience

## Key Contributions

- Comprehensive review of functional neuroimaging in aging
- Discussion of functional reserve and neural compensation
- Integration of connectivity changes with cognitive performance
- Framework for understanding successful aging

## Abstract Summary

This review examines neuroimaging evidence for functional brain changes in aging, focusing on the concept of functional reserve. The author discusses how older adults recruit additional brain regions to maintain performance, and how connectivity patterns change with age.

## Significance

Important synthesis of aging neuroimaging literature, linking brain changes to cognitive outcomes and reserve concepts.
