# UK Biobank Brain Imaging: Structural MRI in a Massive Population Resource

**Authors:** Stephen M. Smith, Lloyd T. Elliott, et al.  
**Year:** 2021  
**Venue:** bioRxiv (later published)

## Key Contributions

- Identification of brain aging modes from population data
- Characterization of brain structural changes across the lifespan
- Relationship between brain aging and health outcomes
- Normative models for brain aging

## Abstract Summary

This paper presents analyses of structural brain changes across the lifespan using UK Biobank data. The authors identify distinct modes of brain aging and characterize how these relate to genetic factors, lifestyle variables, and health outcomes.

## Significance

Provides a comprehensive characterization of normative brain aging at population scale. The aging modes identified provide a framework for understanding individual differences in brain aging trajectories.
