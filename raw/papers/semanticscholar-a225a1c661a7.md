## Abstract

A critical challenge in fMRI-based visual brain decoding is that models trained on data from individual subjects cannot generalize to new participants without extensive retraining. To address this issue, this study proposes a multi-subject transfer learning approach based on whole-brain fMRI data, which not only achieves competitive within-subject decoding performance, but also enables cross-subject generalization via fine-tuning with a small amount of data. In this study, ResNet model is trained on brain imaging data from 6 NSD subjects, achieving a Top-1 retrieval accuracy of 85.8% on the subjects included in the training set. For completely unseen new subjects without any fine-tuning, the model still reaches an accuracy of 2.3%, which is significantly superior to previous whole-brain multi-subject decoding methods (1.0%). More importantly, after fine-tuning with only 5% of the new subject-specific data, the model’s Top-5 retrieval accuracy is improved to 71.5%. In summary, this study confirms that brain decoding models can achieve cross-individual generalization by learning shared neural representations across subjects, which drastically reduces the task’s data requirement per subject and lays a foundation for the practical application of related technologies.
