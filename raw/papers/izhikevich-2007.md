# Dynamical Systems in Neuroscience: The Geometry of Excitability and Bursting

**Author**: Eugene M. Izhikevich  
**Published**: 2007 (MIT Press)  
**ISBN**: 978-0-262-09043-8

## Summary

Comprehensive treatment of nonlinear dynamical systems applied to neuroscience. Provides geometric understanding of neural excitability, oscillations, and bursting through bifurcation analysis—essential for understanding transitions in neural mass models.

## Key Contributions

- Geometric classification of neuronal excitability types
- Bifurcation analysis of resting and spiking states
- Canonical models for bursting and oscillatory dynamics
- Phase space analysis of coupled neuron populations

## Relevance to Whole-Brain Modeling

Understanding bifurcations in single neurons translates directly to population-level models where similar transitions (rest → oscillation → seizure) occur through saddle-node on invariant circle (SNIC) and Andronov-Hopf bifurcations.
