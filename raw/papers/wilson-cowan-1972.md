# Excitatory and inhibitory interactions in localized populations of model neurons

**Authors**: Hugh R. Wilson, Jack D. Cowan  
**Published**: 1972  
**Journal**: Biophysical Journal  
**DOI**: 10.1016/S0006-3495(72)86068-5

## Summary

Foundational paper introducing the Wilson-Cowan model—a firing-rate description of coupled excitatory and inhibitory neural populations. This is the canonical model for population dynamics and the mathematical basis for most neural mass formulations.

## Key Contributions

- Two-population model: excitatory (E) and inhibitory (I) populations with sigmoid activation functions
- Analytical treatment of steady states, limit cycles, and spatial patterns
- Parameter conditions for oscillatory dynamics
- Connection to earlier work by Beurle (1956) and others on population activity

## Mathematical Form

The equations take the form:
- τ_E dE/dt = -E + S_E(aE - bI + P)
- τ_I dI/dt = -I + S_I(cE - dI + Q)

Where S_E, S_I are sigmoid response functions describing population activation.
